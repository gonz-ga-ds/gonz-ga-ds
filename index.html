Our project streamlined the integration of over 470,000 Jeopardy clues from 39 seasons into a powerful data processing system. Computer Vision Project

RealWaste: Trash, Compost, Recyclable Classification Solutions
As part of a dedicated four-member team, I contributed to an innovative project focused on automating waste classification, using the RealWaste Dataset to differentiate between Trash, Recyclable, and Compost. Our primary goal was to significantly reduce human intervention in waste sorting. We employed advanced techniques such as Harris Corner Detection, RGB and HSV color distributions, alongside deep learning models like VGG-16 and EfficientNet for sophisticated feature extraction. This process was further optimized by applying Principal Component Analysis to reduce feature dimensionality, enhancing the efficiency of our data processing. The extracted features were then analyzed using Logistic Regression and Support Vector Machine models to classify different types of waste. Our comprehensive report demonstrates that the integration of deep learning embeddings with traditional image features provides a highly effective and robust method for accurate waste categorization, offering substantial insights into improving waste management systems.

Research methods and statistical techniques

Exploring Difficulty Voting in the 2020 Election
In the 2020 U.S. Presidential election, our project aimed to uncover whether Democratic or Republican voters experienced more difficulties when voting. Through exploratory data analysis and the creation of detailed visualizations, we investigated voting patterns and challenges across various states. The project focused on assessing the impact of campaign strategies, like those observed in the closely contested 2016 election where critical swing states such as Michigan and Wisconsin were decided by razor-thin margins. These analyses provide insights into how strategic campaign decisions and state-level voter outreach could influence voter experiences and difficulties. The findings are crucial for understanding potential disparities in voting experiences between the two major voter groups, offering a comprehensive look at the electoral process and its challenges.
